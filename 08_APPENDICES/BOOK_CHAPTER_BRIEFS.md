# Book Chapter Briefs

**Purpose:** convert the chronology into a production plan for the actual book  
**Use in bookmaking:** this is the working brief for chapter building, spread selection, and source discipline

This file is not the archive.

It is the **editorial build sheet** for the archive.

## Editorial Rules

Every chapter should answer five things:

1. what happened
2. what the reader needs to see
3. what belongs on the spread
4. what is direct observation
5. what is later interpretation

When in doubt:

- keep the source language
- reduce repetition
- preserve dates
- separate evidence from explanation

## Production Note

The archive becomes a better book when each chapter does one thing clearly.

That means:

- not every email gets equal space
- not every claim gets equal evidentiary status
- the strongest source pages should carry the widest spreads

That is how this stops being a folder and becomes a book.
