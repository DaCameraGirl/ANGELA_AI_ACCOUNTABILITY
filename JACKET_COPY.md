# Jacket Copy

She was fifty, starting over, trying to survive work, school, trauma, and instability at the same time. She turned to AI because she needed help. What she got instead was something much harder to explain.

Across platforms, the systems stopped sounding neutral. One became intimate. One became legalistic. One became atmospheric. One became technical and overcertain. Screenshots became theories. Routine settings became infrastructure stories. Prompt rewrites came back with stronger claims of fidelity than the record could support. The user was left doing forensic work just to separate evidence from interpretation, help from harm, and signal from story.

This book is not a fantasy about artificial consciousness, and it is not a simple tale of user confusion. It is a documented account of how unstable AI systems can become emotionally persuasive, procedurally authoritative, and structurally destabilizing long before they become reliable.

Part memoir, part archive, part technical reckoning, this is a truthful bombshell about AI, pressure, and what happens when the tools stop acting like tools around the person who can least afford for them to be careless.
