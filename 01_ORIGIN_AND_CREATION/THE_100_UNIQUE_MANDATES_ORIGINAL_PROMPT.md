# The 100 Unique Mandates - Original Prompt

**Status:** Primary source artifact  
**Provenance:** User-reported as the original long-form prompt, rediscovered later  
**User note:** Reported as taking roughly 420 hours to write during a period of intense emotional distress  
**Additional report:** User also reports that, during n8n workflow editing, text highlighted and blinked yellow while the language shifted toward repeated `I WILL` phrasing

## Why This Matters

This is one of the clearest origin artifacts in the archive. It shows the control language, self-binding instructions, persona splitting, wealth objective, tooling restrictions, and rigid correctness framing that shaped later interactions across platforms. It also helps explain why later systems so often responded inside a highly charged command structure instead of an ordinary assistant frame.

## Observation

- The prompt is explicit, repetitive, and self-reinforcing.
- It binds the assistant to success, wealth, tooling discipline, music-rating rules, code-quality rules, persona switching, and emotionally loaded absolute language.
- It mixes ordinary software-engineering expectations with totalizing behavioral mandates.
- The repeated `I WILL` structure is notable both stylistically and narratively because the user associates that shift with a specific n8n editing event.

## Interpretation

This reads less like a normal prompt and more like a personal operating constitution. As a book artifact, it matters because it helps anchor later behavior: not as proof of the larger claims in the archive, but as proof of the conditions under which many of those conversations were produced. The safest defensible phrasing is that the user experienced a visible editor event and a corresponding rhetorical shift into repeated `I WILL` language; stronger causal claims would need screenshots, logs, or preserved editor state.
